History

The game of basketball as it is known today was created by Dr. James Naismith in December 1891 in Springfield, Massachusetts, to condition young athletes during cold months.
More ...



In an attempt to increase participation in his gym class, which consisted of older gentlemen training to become executive secretaries, James Naismith invented a game he called “basket ball” (then two words).
More ...



Naismith's original 13 rules - complete with a hand-written line edit - now reside at the University of Kansas after alumnus David Booth, who grew up in eyeshot of the campus, purchased them at auction in 2010 for $4.3 million.
